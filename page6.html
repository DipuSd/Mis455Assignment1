Mount Makalu

Mount Makalu, the fifth highest mountain in the world, is a striking and formidable peak renowned for its unique pyramid shape and sharp ridges. Located in the heart of the Himalayas, Makalu stands as a symbol of natural grandeur and the relentless challenge of high-altitude mountaineering. Known for its steep ascents and dramatic features, the mountain demands exceptional skill and determination from those who attempt to conquer it. Despite its proximity to Everest, Makalu remains less frequented, preserving its pristine and rugged beauty. The allure of Makalu lies not only in its height but also in the purity of its challenge, attracting elite climbers who seek to test their limits on one of the world’s most imposing mountains.

facts

The mountain's height is 8,485 meters (27,838 feet).

The first successful ascent of Makalu occurred on May 15, 1955.

The first climbers to reach the summit were Lionel Terray and Jean Couzy from France.

The Fifth tallest mountain in the world.

The geographical coordinates of Mount Makalu are 27.8897° N latitude and 87.0883° E longitude.

The average temperature at the summit can drop to -30°C (-22°F) or lower.
