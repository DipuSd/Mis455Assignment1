Mount Lhotse

Mount Lhotse, the fourth highest mountain in the world, stands as a prominent and awe-inspiring peak in the Himalayas. Often overshadowed by its more famous neighbor, Mount Everest, Lhotse is renowned for its dramatic and challenging terrain. The name Lhotse means "South Peak" in Tibetan, a testament to its close proximity to Everest. Despite being less well-known, Lhotse offers climbers a formidable challenge with its steep faces and perilous conditions. The mountain’s rugged beauty and the technical difficulty of its routes continue to attract elite mountaineers from around the world, each drawn by the allure of its majestic presence and the thrill of its ascent.

facts

The mountain's height is 8,516 meters (27,940 feet).

The first successful ascent of Lhotse occurred on May 18, 1956.

The first climbers to reach the summit were Ernst Reiss and Fritz Luchsinger from Switzerland.

The Fourth tallest mountain in the world.

The geographical coordinates of Mount Lhotse are 27.9617° N latitude and 86.9330° E longitude.

The average temperature at the summit can drop to -30°C (-22°F) or lower.
